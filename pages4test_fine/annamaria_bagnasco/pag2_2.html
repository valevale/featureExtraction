Dr Geraldine Byrne

Memberships and Appointments:

 

Cochrane Collaboration for Systematic Reviews and, Member

European Mentoring and Coaching Council, Member

 

 

Geraldine Byrne is a nurse researcher with a particular interest in people’s experience of health and illness, the ways in which they use health services and how services can be developed to more effectively meet their needs. Her work has focused mainly on Accident and Emergency (A&E) care. Over the past ten years she has researched and published on a number of areas including emergency care nursing, nurse-patient communication, qualitative research methods and ethical issues in research. Her current research explores the impact of NHS Direct on patients’ health outcomes and use of health services.

 

Geraldine is highly committed to using research to inform and enhance practice. She has undertaken a number of systematic reviews, in collaboration with Frances Bunn, Senior Research Fellow in CRIPACC, which have been published in the Cochrane database and other high quality professional journals and supported nurses undertaking evidence-based practice secondments at UH. She is also provides leadership coaching and action learning for nurses and health professionals, working with individuals and groups to improve practice and enhance patient care.
